What is Ripliel?

Ripliel is a browser extension designed to help readers who struggle with line tracking while reading on screen. It creates visual anchors below lines of text, making it easier to find your place and move from line to line.

How It Works

The extension places small, colorful visual markers (anchors) below text at regular intervals. Each anchored line has three markers: at the start, middle, and end of the line.

  • Consistent Patterns: Each anchor's shape and color is determined by the text content, so they remain consistent even when you resize the page or revisit later.
  • Visual Memory: The unique patterns help you remember where specific content is located on the page.
  • Serif Fonts: Optional serif font styling can improve readability for many readers.

Who Can Benefit?

Ripliel may be helpful for:

  • People with dyslexia who experience line-skipping or lose their place
  • Readers with visual stress or Irlen Syndrome
  • Those with attention difficulties (ADHD)
  • Anyone who finds it difficult to track lines on wide screens
  • People reading for extended periods who experience eye fatigue

Research Background

Supporting Studies

Visual Guides and Reading
Studies show that visual guides and rulers can significantly improve reading accuracy and reduce line-skipping in readers with tracking difficulties.

Schneps, M.H., et al. (2013). "E-readers are more effective than paper for some with dyslexia." PLoS ONE, 8(9). DOI

Line Spacing and Tracking
Research indicates that line spacing and visual markers can help readers maintain their position in text, particularly benefiting those with dyslexia.

Zorzi, M., et al. (2012). "Extra-large letter spacing improves reading in dyslexia." PNAS, 109(28), 11455-11459. DOI

Serif Fonts for Reading
Some studies suggest serif fonts may aid reading by providing additional visual cues that guide the eye along the text line.

Moret-Tatay, C., & Perea, M. (2011). "Do serifs provide an advantage in the recognition of written words?" Journal of Cognitive Psychology, 23(5), 619-624.

Visual Stress and Reading
Visual stress affects reading comfort and speed. Visual aids and modifications can help reduce symptoms.

Wilkins, A.J., et al. (2001). "Rate of Reading Test: its reliability, and its validity in the assessment of the effects of coloured overlays." Ophthalmic and Physiological Optics, 21(6), 459-465.

Tips for Best Results

  • Adjust the anchor frequency to match your reading pace
  • Try different fonts to find what works best for you
  • The first line of each article is always anchored to help you start
  • Use consistently to build visual memory associations
